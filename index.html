Information Architecture Work

In addition to thinking and teaching about Information Architecture for the past 8 years, I have also been able to work on projects relevant to creating positive user experiences by optimizing inhouse tools, or creating new ones. Some of my favorite are highlighted below.

University Digital Asset Management System

The communications and public affiars department at a large R1 university adopted a digital asset management system (DAM) to help incorperate partners into their workflow. The system incorperated legacy structures and the wanted to optimize their policy and procedures regarding the informaiton architecture in order to ensure system use. I created the artifacts below and provided additional consulting services. The project is on going as they higher staff in reponce to recommendations I made.

  • This document is the general use and update procedures for the system. It guides not only other policy documents, but also its own updating.

  • This document was created to manage the metadata of objects uploaded to the DAM. It balanced usability, with structure.

  • This graphic is a revised directory structure for the DAM. It had a legacy sturcture that had additional adhoc elements. This saught to minimize the ad hoc nature of suplimental directories, and bring older directories into compliance with newer ones.

Our Knowledge Community

This project was undertaken in support of research efforts of a professor. The professor, wanted an accessable web based directory that could be used to foster communities of practices within different church congrigations. The project required the use of word press, and the Cognizent Form plug-in. Using these constraints I created a website that allowed congrigations to enter informaiton and for members to be able to see information in their church but not others. The website was designed to scale up becoming more complex as the researh project developed. The color choice of these documents was determined by the researcher's desires.

  • This diagram was made to document the desired exprience of using the website. Developing this gave me the frame work to figure out other demands and communicate project requirments to non-technical stakeholders.

  • This diagram defined the requirements for the site related to plugins. It really helped me make choices when I was choosing which plugins to use to build the site. In particular, it helped me to articulate the way in which user login, permissions, and information interacted.

    • This diagram shows the different data types to be captured and used and when and where that information was visable. This formed the basis of the system that I built.
